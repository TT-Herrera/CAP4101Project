Empowering lives, one step at a time.
Emma

Emma, a young adult with physical disabilities, relies on her dog, Luna, to help with daily activities From picking up items to offering balance and comfort in crowded spaces, Luna gives Emma independence and confidence she needs to live fully. Together, they navigate each day, with Luna always by her side-- Emma's loyal helper and constant source of strength.

John Jones, Military LTJG

After serving the military, John struggled with PTSD, making crowded places feel overwhelming. But with his service dog, Max, by his side, he found the courage to take his family to the aquarium. Max provided calm in the chaos, letting John focus on his daughter's joy as they explored sea life together. In these moments, Max wasn't just a helper; he was John's anchor, transforming challenges intro treasured family memories -- one "helping paw" at a time.

Samantha

Samantha, a curious 7-year-old with Type-1 Diabetes, loves animals and can't wait to explore the aquarium with her classmates this Spring. Her dream day? Seeing sea life, coloring what she finds, and feeling just like her friends. While she wishes for a service dog to help her feel safe and aware her on her sugar levels on outings, today she's excited for a colorful adventure where she can learn where and have fun, just like everyone else.

How Service Animals Make a Difference
Daily Task

Many people with disabilities feel reassured with the help and expertise of their fully trained companion, enabling them to accomplish tasks they couldn't before.

Verterans

PTSD service animals are trained to recognize anxiety triggers, provide comfort during distress, and create a calming presence, helping veterans regain confidence and peace.

Kids

Service animals for children are trained to offer compainionship, assit in mangaing emotions, and helping build confidence, enhancing their daily lives and independence.

30% Difference

Service animal owners experience reduced anxiety, fewer emergency visits, and increased activity, leading to an estimated 30% reduction in stress.

Statistic

An estimated 500,000 service dogs are currently assisting people in the U.S., helping with physical disabilities, mental health, and navigation.
